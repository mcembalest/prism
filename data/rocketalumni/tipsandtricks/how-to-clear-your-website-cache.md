What is a Cache?
While searching the internet, your website browser (Chrome/Safari/Firefox/Edge/Explorer) collects bits of information (or code) from the sites you visit and stores them on your device to optimize future page loading speeds.

However, if the cache gets full, your browser's memory becomes overloaded, resulting in some websites taking longer than usual to load or function.

Clearing your cache refreshes browser memory by cutting out those bits of code that were slowing it down.

Clearing the Cache: Safari
Step 1

Click Safari at the top left corner of your screen then Preferences


Step 2

Click Privacy then Manage Website Data


Step 3

Click Remove All OR Hold down the Shift key and click Remove to delete items in groups

Note that Safari will delete both cache and cookies so you will be logged out of any websites that you were previously logged into

DONE!

 

Clearing the Cache: Safari (iPhone)
Step 1

Go to Settings and scroll to Safari


Step 2

Scroll to Clear History and Website Data

Tap Clear History and Data when the popup appears


 


DONE!

 

Clearing the Cache: Android
Step 1

Go to Settings then Apps & Notifications


 


Step 2

Tap on App Info then Google Chrome


 


Step 3

Tap on Storage & Cache then Clear Cache (trash can icon at top)


 


DONE!

 

Clearing the Cache: Firefox
Step 1

Go to the Application Menu then Help


Step 2

Click More troubleshooting information


Step 3

Click Clear Startup Cache then Restart


 


DONE!

 

Clearing the Cache: Edge
Step 1

Go to Menu then Settings


Step 2

Click Privacy, Search, and Services then scroll to Clear Browsing Data

Click Choose what to clear


Step 3

Select a time range of All time

Check Cached images and files

Click Clear now


DONE!

 

Clearing the Cache: Internet Explorer
Step 1

Open Internet Explorer

Click Settings then Internet Options


Step 2

Under Browsing History click Delete


Step 3

Check all 4 top boxes then Delete


Step 4

Click OK, Confirmation is shown below


DONE!
